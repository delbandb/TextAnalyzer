[200~I had chosen to walk the path.
The first was peaceful—lined with trees and grass, bathed in green.
It felt like a park, or a gentle road forgotten by time.
There was no fear. No threat.
Only a quiet joy.
The kind a child feels before the world teaches danger.
I walked with steady strength and unwavering purpose.
Insects hovered in the air, landing softly on my hands, trying to bite—
but they didn’t disturb me.
I calmly caught them, crushed them, and moved on.
There, nestled in the grass, I saw a baby crocodile.
It made no real threat.
Its small body twisted in a strange dance, like it was trying to scare me—
but I wasn’t yet wired to fear it.
I smiled at the absurdity of it.
And passed it by, untouched.
The green path ended gently.
Then came the second.
Dry. Yellow. Sandy.
It felt like another world.
I wasn’t sure if it was I who had changed…
or if this road belonged to a different reality altogether.
The air was heavier here.
Dense. Polluted, maybe.
And yet—it pulled at me.
My heart felt its call.
The paradox of a deep, familiar love…
even for something I knew was distorted.
As I walked across the sand, I felt it:
something alive beneath the ground.
Massive. Coiled. Sleeping.
Its presence undeniable.
Then I saw more.
It wasn’t just one.~
