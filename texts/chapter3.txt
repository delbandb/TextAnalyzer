Beneath the sand, an entire network of giant serpents lay hidden.
Dormant.
Waiting.
And my presence—my energy, my awareness—was waking them.
But I wasn’t afraid.
I was grieving.
It felt like my heart had been swallowed by those same sleeping serpents.
And some silent knowing inside me whispered:
Leave.
I had no choice but to obey.
To leave my heart buried in the belly of that road,
and force my feet to walk away.
So I did—
slowly, cautiously.
But I was torn.
I stepped back.
And looked once more toward the road behind me...
From a short distance, I saw it more clearly:
a place where people lived.
Fragile homes, built from scraps of wood and fabric,
lined the road like forgotten relics.
Old clothes hung from lines—
faded, hollow.
And a white dress swayed gently in the wind.
Something about it caught my breath.
I wondered:
How do they live here, and never disturb the serpents?
Do they even know?
Or have they never looked deep enough to awaken what lies beneath?
But I had looked.
And once you see—
you cannot unsee.
I stood there, frozen at the edge of the road.
My heart still buried in that place.
My feet unwilling to return.
