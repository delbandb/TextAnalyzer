I climbed freely, effortlessly, moving toward the roof.
Step by step, I ascended—no resistance, no fear.
Only the silent knowledge that I was meant to reach the top.
At last, I opened the door that led to the rooftop balcony.
And there she was.
My lover. Radiant.
Sitting quietly at the edge, staring into the sky.
Her aura shimmered—a luminous violet, tinged with deep blue,
almost indistinguishable from the sky itself.
It pulsed gently around her, reaching upward—
as if her soul was in conversation with the cosmos,
yearning to dissolve into the infinite.
She hadn’t noticed me.
Or maybe she had but was lost in something greater.
I approached her gently and whispered, “My love…”
She turned; her eyes filled with that impossible blend—
melancholy and serenity.
A silent ache… and deep acceptance.
She longed to fly.
To become one with the sky.
To leave the building.
To be free.
And yet… she remained.
Her voice, like the wind at twilight, replied,
“I know. Even though I desperately desire…
I am here. For now.
